02

History చరిత్ర

From Koravattu to Korutla

Korutla’s past stretches back over a millennium. Epigraphy from the 11th century has been found at the ancient stepwell (koneru); the town was once a significant Jain centre under the Western Chalukyas, Vemulawada Chalukyas, and Rashtrakutas.

Ancient names

Koravattu, Korapattu, Koravatlu, Koratlu, Kagithalapatla — names echoing Jain and local traditions.

1042–1068 CE

Stone inscriptions (shilashasanam) at the holy pond document the town’s deep historical layers.

Medieval fortification

Six burujulu (watchtowers) guarded a central palace (gadi); connecting walls and a water canal (“Kalvagadda”) recall strategic town planning. Five towers still stand; the Korutla Stepwell remains a landmark of rock-cut engineering.

Faith & syncretism

Jain Tirthankara idols reside within Shree Mahadevaswamy Temple, built in the Shiva tradition — a living symbol of shared heritage. An ancient katar (dagger) was discovered at the temple.

Modern era

After Hyderabad’s integration (1948) and state reorganisation, Korutla became part of Telangana’s story — today a municipal town and rail hub on the Peddapalli–Nizamabad line.

03

Geography భౌగోళికం

On the Deccan plateau

Korutla sits at approximately 18.82°N, 78.71°E, on the Deccan plateau. The municipality covers about 34.12 km² at roughly 287 m above sea level — typical of northern Telangana’s mixed scrub, farmland, and seasonal watercourses.

The surrounding Jagtial district borders Nirmal, Mancherial, Karimnagar, Peddapalli, Sircilla, and Nizamabad — placing Korutla in a network of markets and movement between Hyderabad and the Godavari basin.

  • Climate: tropical semi-arid with distinct wet and dry seasons
  • Economy: strongly tied to agriculture, trade, and services
  • Connectivity: NH 63 passes through the town

Infrastructure ఇన్ఫ్రాస్ట్రక్చర్

Rail, road, and civic backbone

Rail — KRLA

Korutla Railway Station (code KRLA) lies on the Peddapalli–Nizamabad line, linking passengers and freight to wider India. Electrification and express connectivity strengthen the town’s role as a regional node.

Photo: Srikarluckyrail, CC BY-SA 4.0

🚌

Road & TSRTC

TSRTC bus stand serves Hyderabad, Warangal, Karimnagar, Nizamabad, Tirupati, and more. National Highway 63 runs through Korutla.

🏛️

Municipality

Urban services — roads, sanitation, street lighting, and local planning — are overseen by Korutla Municipal Council.

🌾

Agriculture & storage

Cold storage and mandi infrastructure support crop trade — central to the local economy alongside retail and services.

🎓

Higher education

P.V. Narasimha Rao College of Veterinary Science (2008, under PVNRTVU) offers B.V.Sc. & A.H., clinical complexes, and livestock training — a major institution for the region.
